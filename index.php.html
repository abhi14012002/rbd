Bacterical Growth, Mould and Fungus

Excessively humid air containing bacterial elements poses a heightened risk of compromising the integrity of production and storage environments. When relative humidity surpasses the 70% threshold, it fosters the growth of mold’, fungi, and mildew.

Throughout the entire cycle, encompassing raw material handling, production processes, packaging, transportation, and storage, elevated relative humidity exerts a substantial influence on both products and infrastructure. The longevity and overall quality of products can experience substantial enhancements through the implementation of dehumidification measures.

Chemical Reactions

Water vapor, often in the form of moisture, constitutes a critical factor that can initiate undesirable chemical reactions. Notably, lithium, a key component in lithium battery production, exhibits a highly reactive response when exposed to water vapor. Hence, the manufacture of lithium batteries necessitates a controlled environment with extremely low humidity levels achieved through dehumidification.

Furthermore, explosives and propellants are susceptible to moisture-induced chemical reactions. Maintaining precise humidity levels through dehumidification measures is essential to avert such reactions, which could result in product instability, explosion risks, or the formation of hazardous compounds, such as hydrogen gas, with potentially severe consequences.

Corrosion

Corrosion, characterized by the oxidative deterioration of metals, is profoundly affected by the presence of atmospheric moisture. Elevated relative humidity levels lead to the formation of a fine layer of water vapor molecules on the metal surfaces.

Dehumidification effectively curtails relative humidity to a range of 45-50%, successfully arresting the corrosion process. This preventive measure offers a substantial reduction in damage and associated maintenance expenses.

Ice Build Up

The accumulation of ice and frost has significant repercussions on productivity, product quality, and safety measures.

A dehumidification system serves as a paramount solution to uphold the utmost safety standards by effectively preventing ice and condensation. The absence of ice not only prevents worker slip incidents and equipment damage caused by falling ice but also reduces accidents due to impaired visibility caused by mist. The overall climatic improvements further optimize the functionality of evaporators and freezer equipment by averting ice formation, thereby enhancing performance, reducing defrost cycles, and yielding savings in maintenance costs.

Moreover, the elimination of fog and mist within cold storage facilities markedly enhances visibility and safety standards.

Moisture Absorption

Maintaining precise climate control is imperative throughout the production, packaging, and storage phases of hygroscopic product handling. The absorption of moisture has the potential to disrupt the optimal functionality of the final product, as it can significantly impact shelf life when moisture is absorbed during production. Certain products necessitate production and packaging within environments where relative humidity levels remain below 25% RH.

Rehoboth dehumidification systems stand out as the most straightforward and precise method for achieving and maintaining the exacting humidity conditions essential for these processes.

Mechanical Properties

Materials inherently interact with their surrounding environment. In humid climates, materials tend to absorb more moisture from the air, while in arid climates, the opposite effect is observed. This interaction can, in some instances, result in alterations to the mechanical properties of these materials.

Excess moisture can cause paper to lose its rigidity and straight form. Moisture-sensitive adhesives may experience a decline in bonding effectiveness. Additionally, nylon has the capacity to absorb substantial moisture, affecting product dimensions and tolerances.

Product Caking/Sticking

Numerous powders and products exhibit an affinity for attracting and retaining moisture. When stored in silos, bins, or transported through pneumatic conveying systems, this moisture can lead to ingredients adhering to silo walls, causing blockages. The consequences of these issues include reduced productivity, increased production costs, and a decline in product quality.

Dehumidification represents a straightforward and highly efficient approach to prevent condensation, adhesion, or agglomeration. This, in turn, results in enhanced productivity, reduced production costs, decreased downtime, improved hygiene standards, and an overall elevation in product quality. Moreover, dehumidification effectively curtails the occurrence of hammer rash within silos.

Conveying Lines

Hygroscopic ingredients and powders, when exposed to moisture, tend to become adhesive and accumulate within conveying lines. This leads to flow issues that not only disrupt the process but also raise hygiene-related concerns, eventually resulting in expensive downtime.

Pre-drying the air using a Rehoboth dehumidifier guarantees the transportation of products in a dry environment. The outcome is a consistently smooth and trouble-free conveying process with no unexpected production interruptions.

Product Coating

Introducing dry air into coating processes can result in shorter and more consistent processing times. This approach ensures that climate conditions within coaters remain stable throughout the year, guaranteeing optimal conditions, regardless of the season. This, in turn, leads to increased productivity and potential energy savings.

Furthermore, employing a Rehoboth desiccant dehumidification system eliminates the risk of contamination in the conditioning system caused by wet return air. Consequently, you can enjoy uninterrupted, year-round production, potentially even increasing it by 15-20%. Reduced drying times and compliance with Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) regulations are additional benefits achieved through Rehoboth dehumidification.

Product Drying

Typically, the prevalent approach for product drying involves the introduction of hot air to facilitate the evaporation of moisture within the product. However, this method is not without its drawbacks, as the rise in temperature and humidity levels elevates the risk of mold and fungus proliferation. This, in turn, can lead to machinery corrosion, and the excessive heat may inflict damage upon the very product being dried.

The utilization of Rehoboth dehumidifiers offers a solution by maintaining consistent conditions that effectively thwart the onset of these detrimental circumstances.

Spray Drying

Spray drying is a widely adopted method for various products worldwide. However, in hot and humid environments, this process is susceptible to seasonal variations. Fluctuations in ambient weather conditions impact the characteristics of the inlet air supplied to the drying process, resulting in diminished drying capacity, significant process variability, and challenges in drying more delicate products.

The introduction of dehumidification technology establishes stable air conditions throughout the year, enhancing control over the spray drying process. This not only safeguards product quality but also prevents product adhesion. It further minimizes powder loss and maintains plant operational uptime at a maximum level.
